SMS Setup with Twilio

Twilio is a cloud communications platform that allows you to send SMS messages programmatically. Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Create a Twilio Account

1 Go to twilio.com/try-twilio
  • Click "Start your free trial"
  • Enter your email, password, and verify your phone number
  • Twilio offers a free trial with $15.50 credit - enough for testing

Step 2: Get Your Account SID and Auth Token

2 Navigate to the Twilio Console Dashboard

After logging in, you'll see your credentials on the main dashboard:

  • Account SID - Starts with "AC..." (e.g., AC1234567890abcdef...)
  • Auth Token - Click "Show" to reveal (keep this secret!)
Where to find it:

Go to twilio.com/console → Your credentials are displayed at the top of the page under "Account Info"

Step 3: Get a Twilio Phone Number

3 Purchase or get a trial phone number
  • Go to Phone Numbers → Get a Trial Number
  • Trial accounts get one free US phone number
  • Copy this number (e.g., +1234567890)
Trial Account Limitations:
  • Can only send SMS to verified phone numbers
  • Messages include "Sent from your Twilio trial account"
  • Upgrade to a paid account to remove these limitations

Step 4: Configure QuickPing

4 Enter your credentials in QuickPing
  1. Open QuickPing app
  2. Go to Settings → SMS Service
  3. Enter your credentials:
    • Account SID: Your Twilio Account SID
    • Auth Token: Your Twilio Auth Token
    • From Number: Your Twilio phone number (include country code, e.g., +1234567890)
  4. Tap Save Configuration

Pricing Reference

  • US SMS: ~$0.0079 per message
  • Phone number: ~$1/month
  • Free trial: $15.50 credit included

Email Setup with SendGrid

SendGrid is a cloud-based email service that allows you to send emails programmatically. Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Create a SendGrid Account

1 Go to signup.sendgrid.com
  • Enter your email and create a password
  • Complete the account verification process
  • SendGrid offers a free tier: 100 emails/day forever

Step 2: Create an API Key

2 Generate your SendGrid API Key
  1. Go to Settings → API Keys
  2. Click "Create API Key"
  3. Give it a name (e.g., "QuickPing")
  4. Select "Full Access" or "Restricted Access" with Mail Send permissions
  5. Click "Create & View"
  6. Copy the API key immediately - you won't be able to see it again!
Important:

The API key is only shown once. Make sure to copy it before closing the window!

Step 3: Verify Your Sender Identity

3 Set up sender verification

SendGrid requires you to verify your sender identity before sending emails:

  • Go to Settings → Sender Authentication
  • Choose "Verify a Single Sender" for quick setup
  • Enter your email address and verify it
  • Or set up Domain Authentication for better deliverability

Step 4: Configure QuickPing

4 Enter your credentials in QuickPing
  1. Open QuickPing app
  2. Go to Settings → Email Service
  3. Enter your credentials:
    • API Key: Your SendGrid API key
    • From Email: Your verified sender email address
    • From Name: Display name (e.g., your company name)
  4. Tap Save Configuration

Pricing Reference

  • Free tier: 100 emails/day forever
  • Essentials: $19.95/month for 50,000 emails
  • No credit card required for free tier

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a contact?
Go to the Contacts tab and tap the + button in the top right corner. Fill in the contact details and tap Save.
How do I send a reminder?
Tap the Quick Send button on the Home screen, or navigate to any contact and select a template to send. Choose your preferred channel (Notification, SMS, or Email) and tap Send.
Can I create custom templates?
Yes! Go to the Templates tab and tap the + button. You can create templates with placeholders like {name} and {company} that auto-fill when sending.
Why am I not receiving notifications?
Make sure you have granted notification permissions to QuickPing. Go to Settings > Notifications > QuickPing and ensure notifications are enabled.
How do I delete a contact or template?
Swipe left on any contact or template in the list to reveal the delete option.
Is my data backed up?
QuickPing stores your data locally on your device. We recommend using iCloud backup to ensure your data is preserved.
Why are my SMS not being delivered?

Common reasons for SMS delivery failures:

  • Trial accounts can only send to verified numbers
  • Phone number format must include country code (e.g., +1 for US)
  • Insufficient Twilio account balance
  • Recipient has blocked Twilio numbers
Why are my emails going to spam?

To improve email deliverability:

  • Verify your sender email address in SendGrid
  • Set up domain authentication for better reputation
  • Avoid spam trigger words in your message content
  • Ask recipients to add your email to their contacts

Troubleshooting

App Crashes or Freezes

  • Force close the app and reopen it
  • Restart your device
  • Make sure you are running the latest version from the App Store

SMS Not Sending

  • Verify your Twilio credentials are correct
  • Check your Twilio account balance
  • Ensure phone number includes country code (+1 for US)
  • For trial accounts, verify the recipient's phone number in Twilio console

Email Not Sending

  • Verify your SendGrid API key is correct
  • Make sure your sender email is verified in SendGrid
  • Check SendGrid activity log for delivery status
  • Ensure the recipient email address is valid

Notifications Not Working

  • Check Settings > Notifications > QuickPing
  • Ensure Do Not Disturb is not active
  • Restart the app and try again
